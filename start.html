Islam (/ˈɪslɑːm/;[A] Arabic: اَلْإِسْلَامُ‎, romanized: al-’Islām, [ɪsˈlaːm] (About this soundlisten) "submission [to God]")[1] is an Abrahamic monotheistic religion teaching that God is merciful, all-powerful, and unique,[2] and has guided humanity through prophets, (including but not limited to; Muhammad, Jesus and Moses[3]) revealed scriptures, and natural signs.[3][4] The primary scriptures of Islam are the Quran, believed to be the verbatim word of God, as well as the collections believed to be the teachings and normative examples (called the sunnah, composed of accounts called hadith) of Muhammad (c. 570 – 632 CE).[5] Islam is the world's second-largest religion with 1.9 billion followers or 24.9% of the world's population,[6] known as Muslims,[7][A] making up the majority in 47 countries.[8][9]
